You might think that Versailles was more favourable to Germany than Potsdam. However, there were very important differences.

 

 

ATTITUDE TOWARDS GERMANY

 

France was determined to punish Germany for the First World War. This meant Germany had to bear the famous 'war guilt clause'. This caused great resentment within Germany and allowed Hitler to come to power.

 

By contrast, France was excluded from Potsdam. The Big Three were determined Germany should not be unfairly punished. Steps were taken to help Germany recover from Nazi rule.

 

TOUCH ME

THE U.S.A.

 

The American goverment never officially confirmed the Treaty of Versailles. This meant they were not part of the League of Nations. Between 1919 and 1941, America isolated herself and played little part in world affairs.

 

The Big Three recognised this was one of the reasons why the League of Nations failed. They were determined not to repeat this mistake. The U.S. was part of the United Nations from the start.

SUPER POWERS

 

The Great War left all the Great Powers badly wounded. Empires crumbled and old certainties shattered. No new power emerged between the wars.

 

By 1945 it was obvious that two countries deserved a new title: not Great Powers, but Super Powers! These countries were the U.S.A. and the U.S.S.R.. They each had massive military capability. Between them, they occupied most of what had been Nazi Europe. This caused tensions at the the peace conferences and beyond.

 

One week after the Potsdam Conference, the U.S. dropped atomic bombs on Hiroshima in Japan. The U.S.S.R. was furious. In 1946 the U.S. asked the U.S.S.R. to sign an agreement to control nuclear power and weapons in the future. The U.S.S.R. refused and the Cold War started.
