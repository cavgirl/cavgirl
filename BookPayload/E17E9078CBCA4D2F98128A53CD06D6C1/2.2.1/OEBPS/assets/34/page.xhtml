The present Town Hall stands in the centre of Ballymena town, on the site of the old market place. A market-house was built there by the Adair family in 1684, when Ballymena only had 500 people!

 

When this market-house fell into disrepair it was replaced by a new building, known as the Old Town Hall. This served as the administrative centre and meeting place for the growing community. The tower was one of the Seven Towers, but sadly no longer stands as the Old Town Hall was destroyed by fire in February 1919.

 

The Town Hall was rebuilt. In 1924 the foundation stone was laid by the Duke of York, later George VI (Elizabeth II’s father). It was opened in 1928.

 

The Braid (where you are now) was built beside the Town Hall in 2008.
