BALLYMENA CASTLE 

In 1865 Sir Robert Alexander Shafto Adair (the man who dubbed the town the ‘City of the Seven Towers’) started to build a new home (a castle!) on the Ballymena Estate. It took twenty two years to complete. This castle’s towers and turrets (you can see them in the photographs) meant it counted as one of the ‘Seven Towers’.

 

This was the second castle to be built on the site. The Adair family built the first castle in the seventeenth century. It burned down in 1740.

 

The Adairs did not live in their new Ballymena Castle for long. In the early  twentieth century they moved to Flixton Hall in Suffolk, England. This meant they became absentee landlords. By 1955, Ballymena Castle had been unoccupied for some time and was badly damaged by fire. The Tower, along with the rest of the Castle, was eventually demolished. Nothing remains and the site is now the car park for the Seven Towers Leisure Centre.
