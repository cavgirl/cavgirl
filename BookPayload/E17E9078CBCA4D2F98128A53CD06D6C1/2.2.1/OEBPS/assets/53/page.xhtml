First Ballymena Presbyterian Church can trace its roots to 1627. It is said that the congregation built the church amongst themselves, each arriving onsite with the materials in their own carts. When it was finished, the building had such a low ceiling that tall men could reach into the eaves.

 

Reverend Dobbin (minister 1837-1853) rebuilt the church in the nineteenth century with a tower. This tower was one of the Seven Towers, but it was pulled down at the end of the nineteenth century when storms made it unsafe. The church itself has been rebuilt and renovated several times over the years, most recently in 2007.

 

First Ballymena has a special link to another of the Seven Towers. Part of the graveyard attached to the Old Parish Church was set aside for ministers of First Ballymena if they chose to be buried there. This is known as ‘The Dissenters’ Plot’ (because Presbyterians are not members of the Church of Ireland). The last burial there was in 1989 when Rev. Dunwoody’s widow was laid to rest.
