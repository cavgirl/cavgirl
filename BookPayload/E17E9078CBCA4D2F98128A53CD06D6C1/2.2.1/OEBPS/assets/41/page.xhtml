Three things were considered important by Victorian gentlemen such as Sir Robert Adair: property, the law of the land, and the church. You could say all of these were represented in the buildings he chose for his Seven Towers—his own family property, Ballymena Castle; the centre of local authority in the Old Town Hall; and no fewer than three churches, which probably made the list because of their tall steeples and/or bell towers.

 

All Saints Roman Catholic Church is a Victorian Gothic church with a bell tower that became one of the Seven Towers. It was  built on the corner of the Broughshane and Cushendall Roads. The man in charge of building the church was Father John Lynch. It was consecrated in 1860. Another priest, Father Alex McMullan, was responsible for installing the bell. This  weighs thirty-seven hundredweight—well over a ton! Imagine trying to swing that by hand—and incredibly, that’s exactly what happens in All Saints RC Church, even now.

 

This tower still stands today.
