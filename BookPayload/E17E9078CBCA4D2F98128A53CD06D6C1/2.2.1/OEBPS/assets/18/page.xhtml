The Old Parish Church tower is one of three towers that is still standing today. The Old Parish Church was built in 1707 and consecrated in 1721. The tower was added in 1822 at a cost of £320.

 

Although the church was extended a few years later it was still not large enough to cope with the growing congregation. A new church was built in nearby Castle Street on land given by the Adair family.

 

The Old Parish Church was used for the last time in 1855. It was knocked down in 1933— except the tower, which was allowed to stand. It was listed in 2006, preserving it for the future. 

 

It is located along Church Street and has restricted access, opening to the public on Sunday afternoons.
