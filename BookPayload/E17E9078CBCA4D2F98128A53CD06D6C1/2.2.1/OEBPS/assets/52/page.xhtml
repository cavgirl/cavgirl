First Ballymena Presbyterian Church can trace its roots back to 1627.  It is said that the congregation built the church amongst themselves, each arriving onsite with the materials in their own carts.  When it was finished, the building had such a low ceiling that tall men could reach into the eaves.  

 

Reverend Dobbin (minister 1837-1853) rebuilt the church in the nineteenth century, and it was rebuilt with a tower. This tower was one of the Seven Towers. It was pulled down near the end of the nineteenth century after storms made it unsafe. The church itself has been rebuilt and renovated several times over the years, most recently in 2007.

 

First Ballymena has a very special link to another of the Seven Towers. Part of the graveyard for the Old Parish Church was set aside for ministers of First Ballymena if they chose to be buried there. This is known as ‘The Dissenters’ Plot’. The last burial there took place in 1989 when Rev. Dunwoody’s widow was laid to rest.

 

Like many of Ballymena’s towers, the tower on First Presbyterian Ballymena Church was built in the 19th century—only to be pulled down when storms made it unsafe.
