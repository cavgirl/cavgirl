St Patrick’s Church of Ireland was built to replace the Old Parish Church. It was built on land given by the Adair family and intended to hold around 800 people. Colonel Adair laid the foundation stone on St Patrick’s Day 1853. The church was finished two years later. According the minister, William Reeves, this bell tower was 95 feet high—but it never became one of the Seven Towers.

 

That tower and the church were gutted by fire in 1879. They were rebuilt along similar lines for £9,000—£4,000 more than the original building. The rebuilt church still stands today, with a few additions. The bell tower is over 90 feet high. It is one of the Seven Towers.

 

Restoration work was done on the bell tower in the 1980s. The eight bells were melted down and recast as twelve, making St Patrick’s one of only three churches in the whole of Ireland to have twelve bells!
