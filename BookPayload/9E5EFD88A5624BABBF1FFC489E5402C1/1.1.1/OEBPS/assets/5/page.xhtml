In 1919 the statesmen who gathered in Paris were determined to prevent another global war. They set up the League of Nations.

It failed in its primary mission: to prevent war.

Between 1939 and 1945 politicians thought about the League—and why it failed.

Winston Churchill (top) believed the League failed because it allowed small countries to have too much control.

He argued for a new organisation run by the Great Powers, including Britain.

President Franklin D. Roosevelt (bottom), agreed. He thought the League failed because America was not a member. He insisted America be part of a new organisation.

In 1941 the two men signed the Atlantic Charter. This set out their vision for a post-war world, including the establishment of the United Nations.

During the Second World War, 'United Nations' was used to describe the Allied countries fighting against Hitler.
