Sean Lester's opinion was very different. Ireland was not a Great Power or a Super Power. This affected his attitude towards the League of Nations.



For Lester, the League failed because 'the Great Powers had attempted to run the League without consulting the smaller members'. He also said the League failed because its members only used it when it suited them.

In 1919 the statesmen who gathered in Paris were determined to prevent another global war. They set up the League of Nations.

It failed in its primary mission: to prevent war.

Between 1939 and 1945 politicians thought about the League of Nations—and why it failed.

Winston Churchill (bottom right) believed the League failed because it allowed small countries to have too much control.



He argued for a new organisation run by the Great Powers, including Britain.



President Franklin D. Roosevelt (bottom left), agreed. He thought the League failed because America was not a member. He insisted America be part of a new organisation.



In 1941 the two men signed the Atlantic Charter. This set out their vision for a post-war world, including the establishment of the United Nations.
